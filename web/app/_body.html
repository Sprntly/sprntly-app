PRD-042 · Generated by Sprntly · Apr 19, 2026

Fix SMS verification delivery on Android for non-US carriers

Context

Sprntly's weekly brief flagged this as our #1 fix this week. New Android users outside the US drop off at a 43% rate at phone verification — ~2,100 users/week who complete the preceding funnel but never receive a usable SMS code. Historical cohort data shows 47% of activated users convert to paid within 30 days at a $28 blended ACV, translating to ~$14.2K MRR currently leaking. This is a recoverable engineering problem, not a UX problem: the delivery layer is failing silently.

Problem

User view: install, tap sign up, enter phone number, wait. No code arrives within 30 seconds — our measured abandonment threshold. Try again, fail again, leave a 1-star review or close the app. ~87 tickets this month explicitly asked to be verified manually.

Business view: we are burning acquisition spend. Every impacted user represents a fully-paid CAC we never recoup. These are disproportionately our growth markets where new Twilio routes fail most often.

Evidence

  • Amplitude funnel: activation completes at 39% for US Android vs 18% for non-US Android (last 30 days, n=8,421).
  • Twilio delivery logs: median SMS latency is 62s in SE Asia, 54s in LATAM, 41s in India — all above the 30s threshold.
  • App Store / Play Store: 14 one-star reviews in 30 days reference "never got code." All non-US.
  • Intercom: 87 tickets tagged "sms-verification" this month, up 2.1× MoM.

Proposed solution

Tiered SMS delivery strategy with automatic fallback:

  • Primary: Route non-US numbers through regional Twilio senders.
  • Secondary: If delivery not confirmed within 20s, re-send via WhatsApp Business API.
  • Tertiary: If still no delivery after 40s, offer one-tap email verification.
  • UX: Show real-time delivery status ("Sending…", "Delivered", "Trying another method").
Success criteria: Non-US Android verification completion rises from 45% to ≥75% within 30 days. Support tickets tagged "sms-verification" drop ≥60%.

Acceptance criteria

  • Verification screen shows delivery status within 2 seconds of submission.
  • WhatsApp fallback triggers automatically after 20s of non-delivery.
  • Email fallback offered after 40s with a one-tap flow.
  • All three paths log to a single funnel event per-path.
  • Feature flag allows per-region rollout.

Technical requirements

ComponentChangeOwner
Auth serviceAdd WhatsApp delivery adapter + fallback orchestratorBackend
Verify screen (Android)Real-time status UI, method-switching logicMobile
Twilio configEnable regional sender IDsInfra
AnalyticsNew funnel events: verify_sent, verify_fallback_triggered, verify_completed_viaData

Test plan

  • Unit tests per delivery adapter (SMS, WhatsApp, email).
  • Integration tests: fallback chain at 20s and 40s thresholds.
  • Manual QA: real-device test in 5 target regions.
  • Canary rollout: 5% of non-US Android traffic for 48 hours.

Non-functional requirements

  • Latency: orchestrator decision within 50ms.
  • Privacy: phone numbers hashed in analytics events.
  • Reliability: fallback must function if primary provider is down.
  • Observability: per-region completion rates dashboard, hourly updates.
