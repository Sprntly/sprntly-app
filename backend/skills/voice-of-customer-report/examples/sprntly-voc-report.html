Voice of Customer Report

What our customers are telling us to build

Sources: 17 recorded CSM & discovery calls (curated, direct-access) · Coverage: ~15 accounts · Window: Apr 11 – Jun 20, 2026 · Goal & metrics: stop activation churn & unlock expansions — tracked on team-activation rate, gross retention, and net-revenue-retention
⚙ Recommendation basis: volume + churn + partial commercial, selected by fit to the goal metrics above — per-account spend/ARR not connected, so impact is sized on accounts & stated $.

TL;DR

Synthesized from 17 customer and CSM calls across roughly 15 accounts last quarter (April–June 2026), including two churn exit interviews, several at-risk renewal reviews, and active expansion conversations.

Customers are clear that the product itself works — and just as clear about three things standing in their way:

#1They can't get their team onto the product. Inviting people one at a time stalls the rollout before a team ever reaches the critical mass where the habit forms. "I paid for ten and I'm using three — not because it's bad, but because I never finished inviting everyone." — Hatchwork
#2The value never reaches the people who decide renewals. The weekly brief is trapped behind a login, so the execs and partners who won't log in never see it. "My partners decide renewals, and they never once opened the tool." — Marrow & Co
#3They can't expand without access controls. All-or-nothing access means customer security teams won't approve a wider rollout. "Before I add the next fifty, my security team needs roles." — Vela Robotics

What this means for us: these three account for nearly all of the quarter's commercial risk and upside — two of our three churns, a third churn on the brief, and the gated expansions (including a ready-to-close $170K). The throughline: every churned and at-risk account praised the insights themselves. We're not losing on product — we're losing on onboarding, distribution, and governance.
