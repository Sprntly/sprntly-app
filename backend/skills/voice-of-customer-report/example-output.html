Themes — in the customer's words

Hard to get connected

🅗 11 of 17 calls · 19 of 42 tickets · persistent (top theme last quarter too)
Connecting Jira/Linear and reaching the first agent run is where trials stall — described as losing steam, not hitting bugs.
"We were excited on the demo call, and then it took us nine days to see one agent do one thing."CSM call — mid-market account, May (sample)
"I gave up on the Jira connection twice before someone on your team walked me through it."Support ticket #4, Apr (sample)
Impact: activation — 11 of 15 accounts stalled >1 wk 🅗. Blocks value before it lands.

Can't control agent access 🔇

🅗 5 of 17 calls · new this quarter · all 5 are the largest accounts on calls
Admins want per-workspace scoping of what agents read and write. Low volume, elevated on impact: it gates the quarter's biggest stated dollars.
"Legal won't let me turn this on for the platform org until I can fence what the agent sees."CSM call — enterprise account, Jun (sample)
"We'd add three more teams tomorrow. The blocker isn't budget, it's access control."Expansion call, Jun (sample)
Impact: NRR — 🅢 ~$170K expansion explicitly conditioned on this across 3 accounts (stated; verify vs CRM).

Difficult to export PRDs

🅗 8 of 17 calls · 7 tickets · persistent
PMs trust the doc but can't hand it upward — they screenshot into slides, stripping the traceability that made it good.
"My VP is never going to log into Sprntly. I need the doc to come to her."CSM call, May (sample)
"I spent an hour making the PRD, and another hour making it presentable."Interview P6 (sample)
Impact: weekly active PMs — ~1 hr lost per shared doc 🅢. Adoption ceiling, no revenue signal attached.

Hard to review the agent's work

🅗 6 of 17 calls · new this quarter
Users like agent tickets but can't see what the agent inferred vs. what the spec said — so they re-read everything.
"I don't distrust it. I just can't tell what it changed without reading everything twice."CSM call, Jun (sample)
"Show me what it inferred and I'll approve in seconds."Interview P2 (sample)
Impact: agent-run adoption — review time ≈ authoring time 🅢, stated on 4 calls.

R1

Guided tracker-connection flow with a first-agent-run checkpoint

Build a guided setup flow that takes a new account from signup to a connected tracker and a first successful agent run — progress visible at each step, with a human handoff when a connection fails. Attacks the highest volume × severity problem, and activation is a tracked goal metric this quarter.
IMPACTS → activation rate · today 11 of 15 accounts stall >1 wk 🅗 · revenue link 🅘 pending R5
Generate PRDMove to backlog
R2

Workspace-scoped agent permissions (read/write fencing)

Let admins scope, per workspace, what each agent can read and write — so a customer can enable Sprntly for one team without exposing the rest of the org. The silent killer: lowest volume, highest dollars; verify the $170K against CRM before sequencing above R1.
IMPACTS → NRR / expansion · $170K gated across 3 accounts 🅢
Generate PRDMove to backlog
R3

Leadership-ready PRD export (Word/PDF, traceability preserved)

Add one-click export of any PRD to Word or PDF with evidence links and traceability preserved, so PMs can send the document to leadership instead of screenshotting it into slides. Removes a daily-felt workaround with a measurable time cost.
IMPACTS → weekly active PMs · ~1 hr recovered per shared doc 🅢
Generate PRDMove to backlog
R4

Agent-change diff view on generated tickets

Show a diff on every agent-generated ticket that separates what the spec stated from what the agent inferred, with approve-in-place. Turns review from a full re-read into a scan of the changes.
IMPACTS → agent-run adoption · halves the stated review burden if review ≈ authoring today 🅢
