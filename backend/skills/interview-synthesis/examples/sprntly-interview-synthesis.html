What it all adds up to

Across 17 conversations the message was the same: the product works — getting a team onto it and getting its value out to the people who pay don't. Three problems carry nearly all the churn and the upside.

#1Teams can't get onboarded — invites are one-by-one. "I had a spreadsheet of twelve people… I did four and ran out of patience." Problem: teams never hit activation critical mass. Means for us: 2 of 3 churns + a blocked $170K expansion. Do: bulk/team onboarding.
#2Value never reaches the people who renew. "My partners decide renewals, and they never once opened the tool." Problem: the brief is locked in-app. Means for us: renewals die at the top. Do: email-shareable brief.
#3Teams can't expand without access controls. "Right now everyone's an admin and that scares me." Problem: all-or-nothing access fails security review. Means for us: gates the biggest seat expansions. Do: role-based permissions.

Surprises · disconfirming · what we still don't know

💡 Surprises

Product quality was never the complaint — every churned and at-risk account praised the insights. The losses are GTM mechanics, not the product.

⚖ Disconfirming

A few healthy small-team accounts had no onboarding pain — the friction scales with team size. A boundary condition, not a contradiction.

❓ Still unknown

Does bulk invite alone re-activate churned logos? Exact $ on at-risk renewals? Is IdP/SSO sync required, or is CSV paste enough?

📊 Saturation

Onboarding: strong (saturated). Distribution: moderate. Permissions: emerging — validate further.
