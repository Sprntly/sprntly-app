# Module 6 — Voice of customer (sentiment by theme)

**Question:** What do users actually love and hate about each player — including us — by theme?

**Why.** Reviews and social are where structural weaknesses surface in the customer's own words. The pattern that matters is *theme-level sentiment* (pricing, UX, reliability, support, specific features), per competitor vs. you — not an average star rating.

**Method**
1. **Pull reviews** from G2, Capterra, TrustRadius, Reddit, X, and app stores (if mobile). *Free:* these are public; read the 1–2★ and 4–5★ extremes — that's where the signal is.
2. **Tag each by theme** (pricing, onboarding/UX, reliability/bugs, performance, support, key feature areas) and **sentiment** (pos/neg).
3. **Build a sentiment-by-theme matrix:** player × theme, net sentiment + the recurring quote/pattern (paraphrased, not copied).
4. **Compare to us:** where a competitor is hated (e.g. "too complex", "pricing opaque") is your wedge; where they're loved is your bar to clear.
5. **AI-answer sentiment** (ties to module 5): how LLMs *characterize* each brand when asked.

**Output:** sentiment-by-theme matrix per player vs. us, with the top recurring praise/complaint per competitor and the wedges it reveals.
**Hand to module 8:** competitor weaknesses to exploit in product + positioning; our own complaint themes to fix.
**Confidence:** 🅢 soft — sentiment from public reviews is directional and self-selected; note volume and recency, don't over-read a handful of reviews.
