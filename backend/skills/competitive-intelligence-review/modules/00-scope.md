# Module 0 — Scope & competitor set

**Question:** Who actually shapes our buyer's decision — and how deep do we go on each?

**Why this first.** Running 40 data points across every competitor produces a month-stale dump that's obsolete on arrival. Top analysts go *deep on the few that matter, shallow on the rest.*

**Method**
1. **Source the candidate set** from real buyer behavior, not a Google list: who shows up in your lost deals, who customers mention in sales calls, who ranks for your category's high-intent search terms, who's named in your category's review-site comparisons (G2/Capterra "compared to").
2. **Classify each**: direct rival · substitute (a different way to do the job, incl. "DIY / do nothing") · adjacent/future entrant.
3. **Pick the 3–5 that shape decisions** for deep treatment. Everyone else gets a one-line "watch" note.
4. **Set depth per competitor**: which modules to run fully (usually the 1–2 you lose to most) vs. skim.

**Outputs to module 8:** the ranked competitor set + why each made the cut; the "watch list" of the rest.
**Confidence:** the set itself is 🅗 hard if drawn from lost-deal/search data; 🅘 inferred if hand-picked.
