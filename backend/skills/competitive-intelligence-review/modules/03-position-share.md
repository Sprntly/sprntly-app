# Module 3 — Position & share (GE-McKinsey 9-box)

**Question:** Where does each player sit on market attractiveness × competitive strength — and what's the *verb* for each?

**Why the 9-box.** It converts a position map into portfolio decisions. Each cell implies an action: **invest / grow**, **maintain / selective**, or **harvest / divest**. That verb is what a PM can act on.

**Method**
1. **Define axes with weighted criteria** (don't eyeball):
   - *Market attractiveness:* segment size, growth rate, margin potential, strategic fit.
   - *Competitive strength:* share/traction, product strength on the buyer's criteria, brand, momentum (from module 5).
2. **Score each competitor and each of your own segments** on both axes (1–5, weighted). Use real data where you have it; label estimates.
3. **Plot on the 3×3.** Read the verb per cell.
4. **Read your own position vs. theirs** — especially: a high-attractiveness segment where your strength is *currently low* = "invest to build strength" (the wedge), not "avoid."

**Output:** the 9-box with each player/segment placed, plus the invest/maintain/harvest/divest call for each — including for *your own* segments.
**Hand to module 8:** which segments to invest in vs. cede; where an incumbent will clearly out-invest you.
**Confidence:** 🅢/🅘 — placement depends on scored criteria; show the scores so it's defensible, not a vibe.
