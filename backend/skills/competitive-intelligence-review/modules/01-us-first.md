# Module 1 — Us first

**Question:** What is *our* position, who are our users, what's our share, where do we win and lose — and what is our strategy/goal?

**Why this first (before competitors).** Every later finding has to answer "so what *for us*." Without our position and goal as the lens, the analysis becomes trivia. This is the most-skipped, highest-leverage module.

**Method**
1. **Position statement:** category, who we're for, the one thing we're best at, the proof.
2. **Users & segments:** our core segment(s), and which we're losing/not serving.
3. **Share & traction:** our share or a proxy (see data-sources: our traffic/app rank vs. category); honest, even if "we're a challenger with ~0 share."
4. **Where we win / lose:** the JTBD and segments where we clearly win, and where we lose — from win/loss notes if available, else labeled inference.
5. **Strategy & goal:** the explicit market goal this review serves (e.g. "win AI-native teams before the raise"). **This is the judging lens for module 8.**

**Outputs to module 8:** our position, segment focus, win/lose map, and the goal every decision is scored against.
**Confidence:** position/goal 🅗 (you know these); share/win-lose 🅢 unless backed by data.
