# Product Strategy Stack

## What it does
Builds (or audits) a complete product strategy and writes it as a **leadership-ready document anyone can follow** — plain language, a strong TLDR up top, and a glossary for any terms. It covers the full arc a real strategy needs: where we are and where the market's going, the evidence-backed problem, an honest diagnosis, how we stack up against competitors, the goals we're committing to, the bets that move those goals (each with concrete projects on an 18–24 month roadmap), the risks and how we'll handle them, and the explicit ask for leadership. It's **narrative-led** (prose, not a wall of tables), grounded in real evidence (cite or label, never assert), pressure-tested through a built-in **five-lens council critique**, and checked for coherence down the stack (mission → goals).

## When to use / when NOT to use
- **Use** to author a new strategy, refine/critique an existing one, or align strategy to execution.
- **Do NOT use** for the vision statement alone (`product-vision`), the roadmap alone (`roadmap`), goal-setting alone (`okr-nct`), or the competitive study (`competitive-intelligence-review` — that *feeds* the benchmark here).

## Inputs
- **Required:** intent to build a strategy (mission/market/product) OR existing materials to refine.
- **Optional grounding:** a competitive review (feeds the benchmark), business context, current metrics/baseline, roadmap & goals, constraints. *If missing, build top-down and label assumptions; never invent market facts or assert a problem without evidence.*
- **Default horizon: 18–24 months** unless told otherwise.

## Method (methodology)
Grounded in **Rumelt** (diagnosis → guiding policy → coherent action; reject goals-as-strategy), **Roger Martin's Playing to Win** (where/how), **Gibson Biddle's DHM** (bet test), **Hamilton Helmer's 7 Powers** (moat durability), and **Three Horizons** (roadmap). Differentiators: evidence-backed problem framing, a real situation diagnosis, competitor benchmarking, and a built-in council critique.

1. **State of the universe** — the honest situation: where we are (current state, traction, strengths/weaknesses), what we've done and learned, how the market is moving (trends, forces, customer-behavior shifts), and why now. Grounded in real numbers; unknowns labeled.
2. **Frame the problem — with evidence.** What problem, for whom, how big (sized), each backed by cited evidence; separate symptoms from root cause. A problem without evidence is a hypothesis to validate, not a foundation.
3. **Diagnose (Rumelt).** The judgment naming the few critical factors and the true nature of the challenge. The same situation supports multiple diagnoses — name the right one and say why; gate any bet resting on an unproven premise.
4. **Benchmark the competition** on the dimensions the *target customer* cares about (not a feature checklist) → where we win/lose, the white space, the gap-and-opportunity conclusion.
5. **Customers & value proposition** — who we serve, their jobs/needs, the crisp value prop + positioning (and how pricing/distribution express it).
6. **Guiding policy / where-to-play, how-to-win** — the one organizing idea that overcomes the diagnosed challenge; directs and constrains choices.
7. **Set goals.** The measurable destination the strategy commits to: a small set of time-bound goals (with a North Star + leading inputs + guardrails). Each goal stated as "by <date>, <metric> from <X> to <Y>." Goals follow from the strategy and are what the bets must move.
8. **Strategic bets — each ONE coherent thing, mapped to a goal.** Test each on DHM; state **which goal it moves and the expected impact**; list the **projects** under it; give an owner, a metric, and a Now/Next/Later sequence. Trigger-gate any bet whose premise isn't confirmed.
9. **Roadmap (18–24 months) with projects.** Horizons (H1 0–6mo / H2 6–12mo / H3 12–24mo) carrying the **named projects** from the bets, each horizon with the outcome it should produce. Outcomes over feature-dumps, but concrete projects named.
10. **Risks & mitigations (table).** Name concentration risks (a dependency under every bet), second-order effects, and diagnosis risk; each with a mitigation.
11. **What we are NOT doing** — the explicit refusals.
12. **The ask for leadership.** What we need to proceed: decisions, resources/headcount, budget, approvals — each specific, with what it unlocks and by when.
13. **Council critique (built-in — run before finalizing).** Pressure-test through five lenses, surface disagreement, resolve to coherence, iterate until all would sign off: VP (focus/narrative), Director-Growth (metrics/resourcing), Director-Platform (execution/dependencies), Principal (honesty/moat durability), Senior PM (clarity/actionability/reusability).
14. **Coherence audit.** Walk mission → vision → strategy → goals → roadmap; flag any level that doesn't serve the one above; give the 1–2 highest-leverage realignments.
15. **Glossary + plain language.** Write so a non-specialist understands it; define every term of art in a short glossary; expand acronyms on first use.

## Output spec — section order, voice, and format
**Narrative-led: prose is the default; tables only at the benchmark, the bets, the risk/mitigation, and (optionally) goals.** A strong TLDR leads. Plain language throughout; a glossary closes. Recommended **order and the voice for each section:**

| # | Section | What it answers | Voice / words to use |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **TLDR** | The whole story in ~6 lines: the bet, why now, the goal, the ask | Punchy, jargon-free. "We will ___ because ___. Goal: ___. We need ___." |
| 2 | **State of the universe** | Where we are, what we've done, how the market's moving, why now | Factual, present/past tense, cite numbers |
| 3 | **The problem (with evidence)** | What problem, for whom, how big | "The problem is ___ for ___, costing ___." Evidence verbs; cite |
| 4 | **Diagnosis** | What's *really* going on | "What's really going on is ___." One sharp judgment, not a list |
| 5 | **Competitor benchmark** *(table)* | How we stack up on what the customer cares about | Comparative, customer-dimension language; end on the opportunity |
| 6 | **Customers & value proposition** | Who we serve and our promise | "For <who> who <need>, we ___, unlike ___." Plain |
| 7 | **Strategy: where to play / how to win** | The chosen approach | Choice language. "We will win by ___. We play in ___." |
| 8 | **Goals** *(this 18–24mo)* | The measurable destination | "By <date>, <metric> from <X> to <Y>." Time-bound, few |
| 9 | **Strategic bets** *(table)* | The few moves that get us there | "Bet A: <verb phrase>. Moves <goal> by <impact>. Because <DHM>." |
| 10 | **Roadmap (18–24mo)** | When the projects land | Horizon + project nouns. "H1: ship ___, pilot ___ → outcome ___." |
| 11 | **Metrics & guardrails** | How we'll know | North Star, inputs (leading), guardrails; distinguish a proof metric |
| 12 | **Risks & mitigations** *(table)* | What could break + the plan | "If <risk> then <impact>; we mitigate by <action>." |
| 13 | **What we're NOT doing** | The refusals | Pointed, declarative. "We will not ___." |
| 14 | **The ask for leadership** | What we need to proceed | Imperative, specific. "We need <decision/resource> by <when> to <unlock>." |
| 15 | **Glossary** | Plain-English definitions | One line per term; no circular definitions |

Close with a one-line note that it passed the council critique + the coherence audit's single break and fix. Use `templates/strategy-onepager-template.md`. Generic across product types.

## Quality checklist (the bar)
- [ ] **Strong TLDR** up top — the bet, why now, the goal, and the ask, in plain language a non-specialist gets in 30 seconds.
- [ ] **State of the universe + evidence-backed problem + real diagnosis + competitor benchmark** are all present (the front half does real work).
- [ ] **A clear Goals section** — few, measurable, time-bound (by <date>, <metric> X→Y).
- [ ] **Each strategic bet names the goal it moves and the expected impact**, lists projects, owner, metric, sequence; unconfirmed premises trigger-gated.
- [ ] **Roadmap spans 18–24 months with named projects** per horizon, each tied to an outcome.
- [ ] **Risks & mitigations table** names concentration + second-order risks.
- [ ] **An explicit "ask for leadership"** — specific decisions/resources/approvals, each with what it unlocks and by when.
- [ ] **Glossary** defines every term of art; plain language throughout.
- [ ] Narrative-led (tables only where they earn it); passed the five-lens council critique; coherence audit done.

## Known gaps / limitations
- Enforces *coherence*, not *correctness* — a clean strategy on a wrong diagnosis is still wrong; pair with `competitive-intelligence-review`/`market-structure` for market truth.
- The council critique is a structured multi-lens self-review, not a substitute for real stakeholders.
- Goal targets are only as good as the baseline — with no baseline, label the target an assumption to set with data.

## Worked example (full leadership-ready format)
**Input:** "18–24 month product strategy for Facebook/Meta, leadership-ready, readable by anyone." *(public company → financials from filings; competitive review available)*
**Output (abridged to show every section in order):**

- **TLDR:** the bet (route AI spend through ads + the messaging graph while securing attention) · why now (record ad quarter, but first-ever DAP decline + TikTok now permanent) · the goal (sustain ad growth, re-grow under-25 attention, prove the assistant earns — in 24mo) · the ask (staged capex tied to AI-revenue milestones; under-25 attention as a top-line goal; bless the RL→wearables reframe).
- **State of the universe:** ad engine hot (Q1 2026 +33% to $56.3B, impressions +19%, price +12%); $125–145B capex; Meta AI ~600M MAU, ~no revenue; DAP fell to 3.56B (first-ever sequential decline); market shifting chatbots→agents. *(sourced)*
- **Problem (with evidence):** strong cash engine on two weakening foundations — attention (DAP down; under-25 is our weakest, TikTok's strongest) and AI return (unproven vs. $125–145B/yr). DAP decline is fact; the *trend* is a gated hypothesis (Iran/Russia one-offs).
- **Diagnosis:** don't optimize the engine while the ground erodes — "route AI through what already monetizes, secure attention, force the assistant to prove one revenue line — in that order." AI is the means; ads + graph are the moat.
- **Competitor benchmark (table):** win on ad-AI, messaging reach, AI-in-app, early wearables; lose on Gen-Z discovery and assistant-as-destination → opening = the agent where billions already are.
- **Customers & value prop:** people (useful AI inside the apps they live in, not a new destination) + advertisers (AI creative at unmatched scale/ROAS).
- **Strategy:** play in AI-multiplied ads + AI-in-messaging; win by routing AI through assets that already monetize and rivals can't copy.
- **Goals (table, 18–24mo):** North Star = ad revenue growing with impressions AND price both rising; under-25 time-spent declining→growing by mo18; Meta AI revenue/user $0→>0 by mo24; RL loss narrowing as wearables grow by mo24.
- **Strategic bets (table — each names the goal it moves + projects):** A. AI-multiplied ads → North Star (Advantage+, generative creative) Now · B. Defend attention → re-grow attention (Reels discovery-AI, under-25 track) Now→Next, gated · C. Monetize assistant → prove AI revenue (agentic commerce in WhatsApp) Next · D. AI wearables → reframe RL (Ray-Ban Optics) Later.
- **Roadmap (18–24mo, projects per horizon):** H1 Advantage+ default + Reels discovery-AI; H2 WhatsApp agentic-commerce pilot + Ray-Ban Optics; H3 assistant monetization at scale + glasses-as-platform.
- **Metrics & guardrails:** North Star + inputs (under-25 time-spent, AI rev/user) + guardrails (feed-trust score; RL loss vs. units).
- **Risks & mitigations (table):** compute = concentration risk; capex fatigue; generative ads eroding trust = second-order (trust guardrail links A↔B); attention-trend = diagnosis risk (gate Bet B).
- **What we're NOT doing:** no all-AI core feed; no standalone ChatGPT rival; no VR-framed RL; no assistant growth without a revenue target.
- **The ask for leadership (table):** staged capex tied to milestones; under-25 as a company goal; RL→wearables sign-off; a revenue-per-user target on Meta AI with an owner — each with what it unlocks + by when.
- **Glossary:** DAP, Advantage+, agentic AI, ROAS, capex, MTIA, Reality Labs, trigger-gated bet — plain-English.
- **Coherence + council:** one break (goals reward ads far more than AI revenue while spend assumes AI is the future → keep AI rev/user a real owned goal or reclassify the spend); council tightened it (Principal gated the attention diagnosis, Platform elevated compute to concentration risk, VP forced one North Star + the ask, Senior PM added owners/projects/glossary).

*(Same structure runs for a private company — financials use funding/scale proxies; everything else holds.)*
