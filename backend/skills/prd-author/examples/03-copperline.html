Parts-Availability Check at Scheduling

AuthorDavid Mumuni
Context

Copperline is a field service management platform for mid-size HVAC and plumbing contractors (~400 customers): the software their office staff uses to take service calls, schedule jobs, dispatch technicians, and invoice. A job's life today: a customer calls with a problem, a dispatcher diagnoses what's likely needed (often including specific parts), schedules a technician, and the technician drives out — a truck roll. Whether the needed parts are actually on that truck, in the contractor's warehouse, or on order from a supplier lives in separate inventory systems the dispatcher must check by hand, if at all. A job finished on the first drive-out counts toward first-visit completion; a missing part means a second truck roll for the same job.

Problem

Scope assumption: scheduling-time check only — not inventory management itself, and not technician-side mobile workflows.

Jobs are scheduled without knowing whether the required parts exist, so technicians arrive unable to finish. For the business, this drives repeat truck rolls and churn; for the dispatcher, confirming parts means manually checking three systems per job; for the contractor's customer, it means a second appointment for work they expected done once.

Users
  1. Dispatcher — scheduling 30–60 jobs a day; needs the parts answer inside the flow she’s already in, not a fourth system to check.
  2. Owner/operator — sees repeat truck rolls as direct margin loss; needs proof the number moved.
Goal
Primary metricFirst-visit completion rate = jobs completed on first visit ÷ total completed jobs · Baseline: 68%
Projected impact68% → ~74% (+6pp). Chain: ~40% of failed first visits are parts-caused × ~50% catchable at scheduling time.confidence: low–medium
GuardrailsJobs scheduled per dispatcher-hour · scheduling-flow abandonment rate
Hypothesis

If availability for a job's diagnosed parts is checked automatically at scheduling time and at-risk jobs are flagged before confirmation, dispatchers will reschedule or order parts proactively, raising first-visit completion without slowing scheduling.

Non-goals

Inventory management / reordering · technician mobile experience · supplier procurement automation.

Risks

Integration data quality varies by contractor; dispatchers may ignore flags under schedule pressure.

Riskiest assumption — exactly one

Parts are diagnosed and attached to the job before scheduling often enough to matter.

Pre-mortem: It's six weeks later and this failed because most jobs had no parts list at scheduling time, so the check never fired. The earliest signal was low check-trigger rates in week-one telemetry. We should have measured parts-list attachment rate before building.

Alignment

Directly attacks the churn driver named in exit interviews.

Rollout

5 design-partner contractors → cohort A/B on flag visibility.

Done-when

Check fires on ≥60% of parts jobs and first-visit completion is measurable against baseline.
